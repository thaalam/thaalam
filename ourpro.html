01

Conventional musical instruments with keyboards, such as a piano or a harmonium, have keys of uniform width, and hence, cannot be distinguished by a visually impaired individual. The generated sound can be perceived only by ears but cannot be sensed by individuals with hearing disability.

02

If provided with an instrument designed specifically for their needs and with consideration to their limitations, the physically challenged not only can enjoy music. Thus, there is a need for a musical instrument for the physically challenged, which eliminates shortcomings of known prior art.

03

We came up with a design of an instrument of a keyboard type for the disabled. It enables creating different vibrations corresponding to the different swarams, i.e., musical notes, which can combine together to play a harmony.
